About

Why I build.

I've spent 15 years at the point where user needs collide with business reality and technical complexity — energy platforms, PropTech at scale, banking in emerging markets, enterprise systems that power things people depend on.

What I've learned: the hardest part is almost never the technology. It's understanding the person at the end of the process well enough to build something they'll actually use. That's what I keep coming back to, and what I think makes AI genuinely interesting right now — not the demos, but the hard human problems underneath them.

I'm looking for collaborators who get excited about problems before solutions. Who'd rather ship something imperfect and learn than spend another month in planning. If that sounds like you — I want to hear what you're building.
