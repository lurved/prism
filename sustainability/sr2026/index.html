About SP Group

Leading Utilities Provider in Asia Pacific

SP Group is the leading utilities provider in the Asia Pacific region, delivering reliable electricity and gas services and enabling a low carbon, smart energy future through its sustainable solutions. SP Group owns and operates electricity and gas transmission and distribution networks in Singapore and Australia.

As Singapore's national grid operator, SP Group serves around 1.7 million industrial, commercial, and residential customer accounts with world-class network reliability. Building on over 30 years of powering Singapore since 1995, SP Group continues to transform with resilience under steady leadership.

Beyond Singapore, SP Group steadily expands into China, Thailand, and Vietnam, providing integrated sustainable energy solutions including district cooling and heating, renewable energy, EV charging infrastructure, and digital energy platforms.

About This Report: SP Group's 7th sustainability report, covering FY ended 31 March 2026. Aligned with IFRS S1 & S2 and the GHG Protocol. A phased approach to external assurance is underway in line with ACRA requirements.

In 2025, SP Group completed its DMA, evaluating impacts on the economy, environment and people (impact materiality) and the potential financial effects of sustainability-related risks and opportunities (financial materiality).

Sustainability Governance

SP Group has established a clear governance structure led by the Board with dedicated oversight from the Board Sustainability Committee.

Ultimate Responsibility
Board Members
Oversight of SrROs, CrROs and long-term sustainability strategy
Board Level
Board Sustainability Committee (SC)
Oversees progress against sustainability strategy and CrROs. Convenes quarterly. SC Chair updates Board after each meeting.
Management Level
Executive Leadership Team (ELT)
Senior management steering growth and development. Engaged on material matters at least quarterly.
Sustainability Team
Coordinating disclosures, policies & metrics. Sustainability business partners assigned to each BU.
Sustainability Taskforce
Cross-functional hub with BU representatives. Meets monthly to review progress and key issues.

The Board holds ultimate responsibility for sustainability and climate-related oversight, including SrROs and CrROs. The Board formed the Sustainability Committee (SC) to oversee sustainability- and climate-related disclosures.

The SC reviews and recommends related strategies, plans, commitments, and policies, and oversees the execution of Board-approved initiatives. The SC convenes quarterly.

SP Group provides ongoing training for the Board, SC, and management. In 2025, the SC and ELT received training on IFRS S1 and S2 disclosure requirements, emissions accounting fundamentals, and climate resilience.

The Board evaluates decarbonisation pathways for impact on operational resilience, weighing near-term costs against long-term risk mitigation and strategic objectives.

Management is responsible for implementing Board-approved sustainability strategy and embedding material sustainability and climate-related risks into SP Group's ERM, internal control, and business processes.

Management establishes processes, controls and reporting mechanisms integrating sustainability into business planning, capital investment evaluation, and operational decisions.

The Sustainability Team coordinates sustainability disclosures, supports enterprise risk management, and delivers training to build organisational capability.

Sustainability-related KPIs (service reliability, cybersecurity, safety, workforce) are embedded in executive and senior management remuneration, including at Group level.

The Sustainability Taskforce serves as a collaborative hub of BU and function representatives, meeting monthly to review progress and key issues.

SP Group manages risks through its Enterprise Risk Management and governance frameworks. Risk owners within the Corporate Office and each Principal Operating Subsidiary (POS) apply the ERM methodology to assess and prioritise risks.

SP Group adopts both top-down (guidance from BRMC and Management, including risk appetite and limits) and bottom-up (employees escalate day-to-day risk issues) approaches.

Sustainability and climate-related risks are identified through market research, regulatory updates, expert insights, and climate scenario analysis. Significant risks are escalated to the Board Risk Management Committee (BRMC).

Climate scenario analysis identifies and assesses physical and transition risks over different scenarios and time horizons. Results inform the ERM process and are integrated for ongoing monitoring.

Climate-related opportunities are managed through existing business and strategy processes — exemplified by the Sustainable Energy Solutions (SES) business segment.
